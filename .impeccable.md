# Design Context

## Users

**Primary**: Professional river guides (B2B) purchasing access to real-time river conditions.
**Secondary**: Outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers seeking immersive river experiences.
**Context**: Users evaluate conditions remotely, decide on trips, and may subscribe for ongoing access. Need confidence in data quality and reliability.

## Brand Personality

**Rugged & Meditative** — Grounded in nature, calm in execution.
**Tone**: Premium but not pretentious. Technical but not cold. Adventurous but not extreme.
**Emotional goal**: Conveys the serene power of wild rivers; premium access to pristine places.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: Both light and dark modes. Light with earthy tones default (feels organic, premium); dark for immersive viewing (lets the river video breathe).
- **Palette**: Earthy, nature-inspired — warm sepia borders, muted earth tones, subtle greens. Avoid harsh blacks, neon accents, or overly saturated colors.
- **Typography**: Inter for clarity and legibility. Generous tracking on labels, tight tracking on display text.
- **References**: Apple product pages (premium restraint), National Geographic (adventure + education), with meditation app calm.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid Twitch/streaming platform aesthetics, dark-mode-only tech vibes, or overly rustic "outdoorsy" clichés.

## Design Principles

1. **Immersive video, functional chrome** — The river is the star. UI recedes; controls appear on hover/interaction.
2. **Premium through restraint** — High-end feel comes from whitespace, typography, and subtle details, not decoration.
3. **Technical transparency** — Specs and details signal quality and trust. Be honest about what you're delivering.
4. **Earth-toned warmth** — Colors evoke natural environments. Sepia, sage, stone, and slate, not sterile grays or aggressive blacks.
5. **Calm authority** — Interface conveys confidence without urgency. Users are making leisure decisions, not emergency ones.
